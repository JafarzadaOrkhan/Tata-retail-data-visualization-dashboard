# 📊 Tata retail data visualization dashboard

## 🚀 Project Overview

This project presents an interactive **Power BI dashboard** built to analyze sales performance for the year **2011**. The goal was to transform raw transactional data into meaningful business insights through effective data visualization.

The dashboard focuses on revenue trends, customer performance, country-level analysis, and global sales distribution.

---

## 📌 Key Visualizations

### 1️⃣ Revenue Trend by Month (2011)

* **Type:** Line Chart
* **X-axis:** Invoice Date
* **Y-axis:** Revenue *(Quantity × Unit Price)*
* **Filter:** Year = 2011

This visualization highlights monthly revenue patterns and helps identify seasonality and peak sales periods.

---

### 2️⃣ Revenue & Quantity by Country

* **Type:** Clustered Bar Chart
* Displays both **Revenue** and **Quantity** side-by-side for comparison
* **Top 10 countries** included
* **United Kingdom excluded** for better comparative analysis

This chart allows clear comparison of sales volume and revenue contribution across countries.

---

### 3️⃣ Top 10 Customers by Revenue

* **Type:** Column Chart
* **Metric:** Revenue by CustomerID
* **Filters Applied:**

  * Top 10 customers
  * Descending order
  * Blank CustomerIDs excluded

This visualization identifies the most valuable customers driving the business.

---

### 4️⃣ World Map of Units Sold

* **Type:** Map Chart
* Shows total units sold across different countries
* **United Kingdom excluded**
* Includes **data labels** for better readability

This map provides a global perspective on product demand and geographic distribution.

---

## 🛠️ Tools & Technologies

* Power BI
* Data Modeling
* DAX (for calculated measures)
* Data Cleaning & Transformation

---

## 📈 Key Insights

* Clear seasonal trends observed in 2011 revenue
* A small group of customers contributes significantly to total revenue
* Certain countries dominate in both sales volume and revenue
* Geographic visualization reveals strong regional demand patterns

---

* Country
* Quantity
* UnitPrice
